This scenario reflects high rent growth, based on the top 10% of historical changes in rent.
Similar outcomes have occurred in roughly 1 out of 10 years between 1983 and 2025.

This represents a high-pressure scenario, where rent increases significantly faster than typical historical levels.

The model uses the Consumer Price Index (CPI) – Rent of Primary Residence, averaging monthly values into yearly figures and calculating year-over-year percentage changes by comparing each year to the previous one.

For example, if the index rises from 100 to 103, this corresponds to a 3% annual increase in rent. This is repeated for all years to build the historical distribution.

These results are based on historical data and are not predictions of future rent changes.
