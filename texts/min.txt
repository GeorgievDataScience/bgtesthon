This scenario reflects the lowest observed rent growth in the historical data between 1983 and 2025.
Such outcomes are rare and not typical, representing unusually low or near-zero rent increases.

This can be seen as an extreme best-case scenario, not commonly sustained over long periods.

The model uses the Consumer Price Index (CPI) – Rent of Primary Residence, converting monthly data into yearly values and calculating year-over-year percentage changes by comparing each year to the previous one.

For example, if the index rises from 100 to 103, this corresponds to a 3% annual increase in rent. This process is repeated across all years to build the full distribution.

These results are based on historical data and are not predictions.
