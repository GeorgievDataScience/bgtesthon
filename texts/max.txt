This scenario reflects the highest observed rent growth in the historical data between 1983 and 2025.
Such outcomes are rare and represent extreme conditions, where rent increases sharply over time.

This represents a worst-case type scenario, not typically sustained but possible under certain economic conditions.

The model uses the Consumer Price Index (CPI) – Rent of Primary Residence, averaging monthly values into yearly figures and calculating year-over-year percentage changes by comparing each year to the previous one.

For example, if the index rises from 100 to 103, this corresponds to a 3% annual increase in rent. This calculation is repeated across all years to build the historical distribution.

These results are based on historical data and are not predictions of future rent changes.
