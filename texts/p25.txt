Similar outcomes have occurred in roughly 1 out of 4 years between 1983 and 2025.

This represents a favorable scenario, where rent increases remain below typical historical levels.

To estimate this, the model uses the Consumer Price Index (CPI) – Rent of Primary Residence. Monthly values are averaged into yearly figures, and each year is compared to the previous one to calculate the year-over-year percentage change.

For example, if the index rises from 100 to 103, this corresponds to a 3% annual increase in rent. This calculation is repeated for each year to build a distribution of historical changes.

These results are based on historical data and show what could happen if future trends resemble the past—they are not predictions of future rent changes.
