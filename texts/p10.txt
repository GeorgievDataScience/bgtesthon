This scenario reflects very low rent growth, based on the bottom 10% of historical changes in rent.
Similar outcomes have occurred in roughly 1 out of 10 years between 1983 and 2025.

This represents a best-case type scenario, where rent increases remain unusually low compared to historical norms.

To estimate this, the model uses the Consumer Price Index (CPI) – Rent of Primary Residence, which tracks rent changes over time. Monthly values are averaged into yearly figures, and each year is compared to the previous one to calculate the year-over-year percentage change.

For example, if the index rises from 100 to 103, this corresponds to a 3% annual increase in rent. This calculation is repeated for each year to build a full distribution of historical changes.

These results are based on historical data and show what could happen if future trends resemble the past—they are not predictions of future rent changes.
