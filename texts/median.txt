This scenario reflects typical rent growth, based on the median of historical changes in rent.
This means that about half of the years between 1983 and 2025 experienced higher growth, and half experienced lower growth.

This represents a common real-world outcome, aligned with long-term historical patterns.

The model uses the Consumer Price Index (CPI) – Rent of Primary Residence, averaging monthly values into yearly figures and comparing each year to the previous one to calculate year-over-year percentage changes.

For example, if the index rises from 100 to 103, this corresponds to a 3% annual increase in rent. This process is repeated across all years to build the historical distribution.

These results are based on historical data and show what could happen if future trends resemble the past—they are not predictions.
