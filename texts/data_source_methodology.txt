The application uses data from the US Consumer Price Index (CPI), specifically from the category Housing -> Rent of Primary Residence. This index measures how rental prices change over time--whether they increase or decrease compared to previous years.

In its current form, the index has been in use since the early 1980s. It is calculated by the Bureau of Labor Statistics. The base period is 1982-1984, which is set at 100 points.

Example:
If the index for "Rent of Primary Residence" is 200 points in 2020, this means that rental prices have doubled compared to the base period (1982-1984).

This provides us with an official and reliable data source that has been tracking rental price movements on a monthly basis for about 50 years. Using the monthly data, we can calculate year-over-year changes. Based on these annual changes, we construct our four main scenarios.
